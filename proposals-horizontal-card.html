Horizontal Base Card — 5 Proposals

Design exploration for iGraal's retailer page offer cards. Each proposal solves a different UX problem.

Proposal A

Urgency-First

Redesigns visual hierarchy around time pressure and deal value. Expiring deals are impossible to miss — a color-coded edge strip + pulsing countdown badge creates scan-and-act behavior.

⏰

What changed

Left edge color strip signals urgency (red/amber/green). Expiry badge pulses for <24h deals. Value amount is 36px — the dominant visual element.

🎯

UX Rationale

Users scanning a retailer page need to quickly spot deals that expire soon. Time-coded strips let them triage instantly without reading every card.

📊

Expected Impact

Higher conversion on time-limited offers. Reduced "missed deal" frustration. Natural list ordering by urgency becomes visually obvious.

Proposal B

Social Proof

Injects trust signals directly into the card — how many people used a deal, trending indicators, and avatar stacks. Converts uncertainty into confidence.

👥

What changed

Added "X osób skorzystało" counter, mini avatar stack, popularity badge ("Popularne 🔥"), and a "Zweryfikowano" verified label. Badge area is now gradient-filled.

🧠

UX Rationale

Cashback users are inherently skeptical — "does this actually work?" Social proof (others used it) and verification signals reduce hesitation and build trust.

📈

Expected Impact

Higher click-through on lesser-known offers. Reduced bounce from "I don't trust this" sentiment. Viral loop: popular deals become more popular.

Proposal C

Visual Brand

Transforms the card into a retailer-branded surface. A bold left accent zone carries the retailer's identity and the deal value, making each card feel like it "belongs" to that shop.

🎨

What changed

120px left brand zone with gradient background, retailer logo, and large offer value. Color-coded per offer type. Card feels owned by the retailer.

🏪

UX Rationale

When browsing a retailer page (e.g. /allegro), users expect brand context. A branded card surface increases perceived legitimacy and emotional connection to the retailer.

💡

Expected Impact

Stronger retailer page identity. Works especially well when iGraal can match retailer brand colors. Higher dwell time on retailer pages.

Proposal D

Compact Dense

Shrinks vertical footprint by ~40%. Users see more offers without scrolling — optimized for mobile and for retailers with 10+ active offers. Every pixel earns its place.

📱

What changed

Reduced padding (12px vs 24px), smaller badge (56×44px), single-line description, no "Sprawdź warunki" link (moved to card tap), border instead of shadow.

📐

UX Rationale

On mobile, a retailer with 15 offers means 15 scrolls with the current card. The compact variant shows 6-7 offers in one viewport — dramatically improving scannability.

⚡

Expected Impact

More offers visible = higher discovery rate. Faster scanning = quicker decisions. Works beautifully on mobile where vertical space is premium.

Proposal E

Premium Elevated

Adds depth, motion, and polish. Subtle shadows, gradient CTAs, glow effects on hover, and refined spacing make the card feel high-end. For when iGraal wants to feel like a premium savings platform, not a coupon site.

✨

What changed

20px border-radius, layered shadows, gradient CTA with glow, elegant value zone with radial glow, divider line, larger typography, spring-physics hover (translateY -4px).

💎

UX Rationale

Cashback sites often look "deal-ish" — cheap, crowded, untrustworthy. Elevating the card's visual quality positions iGraal as a premium partner, not a discount aggregator.

🎯

Expected Impact

Brand perception lift. Users feel they're getting exclusive deals, not scraping for coupons. Hover micro-interactions create delight and encourage exploration.
